Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to convert light energy into chemical energy. The process occurs in the chloroplasts, where chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and uses it to transform carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. The light-dependent reactions require direct sunlight to produce ATP and NADPH, while the Calvin cycle (light-independent) uses these molecules to synthesize glucose from carbon dioxide.

AI Explanation

Think of a plant like a solar-powered factory. The chloroplasts are the solar panels on the roof. When sunlight hits these panels (powered by chlorophyll), the factory goes into action. It takes in carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the air and water (H₂O) from the ground, and transforms them into glucose (sugar = food for the plant) and oxygen (which it releases as a byproduct).


The process has two main stages:

1️⃣ Light-dependent reactions — These are like the factory's morning shift. They can only run when the sun is out. They capture sunlight and create energy-carrying molecules called ATP and NADPH.

2️⃣ Calvin cycle — This is the night shift. It doesn't need direct sunlight. It takes the ATP and NADPH made earlier and uses them to build glucose from carbon dioxide. Think of this as the assembly line that packages the final product.
